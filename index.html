About the Blog:

I tend to learn in bursts, diving deep into an idea, building something half-polished, then moving on once the next question starts tugging at my attention. That pattern has taken me through machine learning projects, research papers, half-filled notebooks, and more experiments than I can neatly summarize. What it hasn’t always given me is continuity. This blog is my attempt to fix that. Writing forces me to slow down, connect threads, and turn scattered exploration into something coherent and reusable. Think of it less as a showcase and more as a working log: a place to reason in public, document what worked (and what didn’t), and impose a bit of structure on an otherwise restless way of learning. If it helps me think more clearly tomorrow than I did today, it’s doing its job.

About Me:

I’m Anant, a sophomore in Electrical Engineering at IIT Kanpur. I’m broadly interested in machine learning and robotics I enjoy working through ideas hands-on, whether that’s implementing models from scratch, experimenting with research directions, or exploring problems that sit between theory and practice.
